Dr. Nicolás Araneda Hinrichs works within cognitive neuroscience of language and is currently a visiting scholar at de Institute for Applied Linguistics and Translatology of de Universität Leipzig, where he is pursuing his Habilitation (on semantic framing through both behavioral and neurophysiological paradigms); stemming from biological anthropology, he borrows its cultural perspective and ethnographic stance in order to also critically work across this niche through the questioning of its epistemic and ontic roots as discursive practice and global mode of knowledge production. This endeavour will come forth in a publication titled “Materialism on Social Cognition”, co-authored with Noah Guzmán.

He is an alumnus of the Universidad de Concepción, where he underwent his BSc and PhD.
