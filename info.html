Dr. Nicolás Araneda Hinrichs works within cognitive neuroscience of language and is currently a visiting scholar at de Institute for Applied Linguistics and Translatology of de Universität Leipzig, where he is pursuing his Habilitation (the institute's director, Prof. Oliver Czuulo, is his current advisor); for this purpose he just founded a research group with Dr. Maryam Foradi to carry out an agenda that combines neurocognitive experiments with computational corpus linguistics on the basis of both primes from Wierzbicka´s Natural Semantic Metalanguage frames from the FrameNet initiative.

Stemming from biological anthropology, he borrows its cultural perspective and ethnographic stance in order to also critically work across the cognitive niche through the questioning of its epistemic and ontic roots as emerging from discursive practices and global mode of knowledge production. This endeavour will come forth in a series of publications redacted with fellow peers. The first one was single-authored and released mid-September 2020 as a research article in a Special Issue of the Journal Frontiers in Neuroscience titled "(New) Realist Social Cognition".

Nicolás Araneda Hinrichs received his PhD in Psycholinguistics from the University of Concepción through the Chilean National Agency for Research and Development (ANID/CONICYT). He also holds a Licenciatura (German equivalent: Diplom) in Physical (Biological) Anthropology from the same institution. For his Dissertation, Nicolás investigated the so-called reversal of the concreteness effect of nouns in Chilean Spanish from a 4E Cognition based perspective. During his study he also performed a research stay at the Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics in Nijmegen, The Netherlands. He also currently works as a freelancer analyst for The Economist Intelligence Unit "DII Healthcare" and recurrently serves as manuscript revisor for diverse journals, publishing houses and conferences.
